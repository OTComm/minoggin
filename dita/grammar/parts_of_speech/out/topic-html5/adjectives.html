Adjectives

Summary

Adjectives tell us more information about nouns and pronouns.

Explanation

There are words that are categorically always possessive adjectives they function exclusively to denote possession or ownership of other nouns; that is, there are some words that are categorically possessive adjectives, their only function is to modify other nouns by mapping that noun to a possessor noun. Many other types of words can function as adjectives without changing their grammatical category. For example, when a proper nouns used descriptively.

Examples

  1. {Her} sweater was lost.
  2. The {red} apples were sold out.
  3. {Those} birds are not real.
  4. She left him {four} cookies
