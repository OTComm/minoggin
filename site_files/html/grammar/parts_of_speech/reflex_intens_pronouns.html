Summary

Reflexive pronouns reflect back to the subject.
Intensive pronouns emphasize the subject.

Explanation

Reflexive pronouns are used when the subject and the object of the sentence are the same person or thing. They play the role of the object, reflecting back to the subject.
Intensive pronouns are used to emphasize the subject of the sentence and are not essential to the sentenceâ€™s meaning but are used to add emphasis to the noun or pronoun they are intensifying.

Examples

  • Reflexive
    1. He taught {himself} to play the guitar.
    2. They cannot do it {themselves}.
    3. I can do it {myself}.
  • Intensive
    1. He {himself} was the only one who could do it.
    2. They {themselves} are to blame for what they allowed to happen.
    3. We {ourselves} are the stewards of our inheritance.
