Summary

Personal pronouns replace Proper Nouns or Common Nouns that were previously mentioned.

Explanation

Personal pronouns replace Proper Nouns or Common Nouns that were previously mentioned. The form of a personal pronoun changes depending on the [[Grammatical Person]] and [[Case]]. Replacement Function: Personal pronouns (like "he," "she," "it," "they") are used to replace nouns that have already been mentioned in a conversation or text. These can be proper nouns (specific names like "John" or "Mary") or common nouns (general names like "teacher" or "apple"). Changes in Form: The form of personal pronouns changes based on: Grammatical Person: First person (I, we), second person (you), and third person (he, she, it, they). Position and Cases: Subjective case (used as the subject of a sentence, e.g., "He runs fast."). Objective case (used as the object of a sentence, e.g., "I saw her yesterday."). Possessive case (shows ownership, e.g., "Their car is new."). They and Them: When the identity of a person is unknown, they and them are used in place of he or she. Examples

  1. {I} am sure this is not what {we} discussed.
  2. {You} should have been there.
  3. {He} saw that {she} was selling the last one {they} had and offered to buy {it}.
  4. {They} had to wait for the next flight because there was only one seat available.
  5. I couldn't see who it was because {they} ran quickly into the darkness.
  6. I don't know who did this, but I hope someone catches {them}.
