Summary

Relative pronouns connect extra information that is related to a noun or pronoun to the rest of the sentence.

Explanation

Interrogative pronouns are used to form questions, to interrogate, and gather information about specific elements in a sentence. Words like “who,” “whom,” “whose,” “which,” and “what” are all interrogative pronouns. Each one serves a distinct function based on the type of information being requested:

  • “Who” asks about people as the subject of the question.
  • “Whom” asks about people as the object of the question.
  • “Whose” asks about possession or ownership.
  • “Which” asks for a specific choice among a set.
  • “What” asks for general information or clarification.

Examples

  1. {Who} does the book belong to?
  2. To {whom} does the book belong?
  3. {Whose} book is it?
  4. {Which} one of my books is missing?
  5. {What} gave you the right to take my book?
