Summary

Determiners are words placed in front of nouns to clarify quantity, possession, specificity, or definiteness.

Explanation

Determiners are words placed in front of nouns to clarify quantity, possession, specificity, or definiteness. They modify nouns to indicate aspects like who owns something, how much of something there is, or which particular items are being discussed.

Examples

  1. {The} apple was juicy. (Article)
  2. {This} pen is better than {that} one. (Demonstrative)
  3. {My} friend lost {her} keys. (Possessive)
  4. {Some} people prefer quiet places. (Quantifier)
  5. {Three} students won the prize. (Number)
  6. {Each} participant received a certificate. (Distributive)
  7. {Which} book do you think is better? (Interrogative)
  8. {A piece of} cake would be nice. (Partitive)
  9. {All} the cookies were eaten. (Predeterminer)
