Summary

An indefinite article introduces a noun without specifying a particular one.

Explanation

There are two indefinite articles in English. “A” and "an" function as referents to a non-specific noun, indicating any member of a group or class. Simply put, **an** indefinite article introduces a noun without specifying a particular one.

Examples

  1. I haven't heard this song since {a} time I was biking down by Fort Snelling.
  2. {A} word is an adjective if modifies {a} noun to tell us something about the noun it modifies.

Grammar Tips

"A" is used before words that begin with a consonant sound, and "an“ is used before words that begin with a vowel sound. For example, "A kidney donor will be here soon." "An organ donor will be here soon."
