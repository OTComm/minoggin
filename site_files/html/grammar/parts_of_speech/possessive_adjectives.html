Summary

Possessive adjectives directly modify nouns to specify who the noun is owned by or associated with.

Explanation

Possessive adjectives directly modify nouns to specify who the noun is owned by or associated with. Possessive adjectives include, "my," "your," "our," "his," "her," "its," and "their." Unlike possessive pronouns, which replace the noun and can stand alone in reference to it, possessive adjectives always immediately precede and modify a specific noun.

Examples

  1. You don't like {her} attitude.
  2. That is {my} dollar.
  3. {Their} ideas are fascinating.
  4. {Our} house is very old.

Grammar Tips

Possessive adjectives are always positioned directly before the noun they modify, signaling ownership or a relation to the noun. For example, in "That is my dollar," "my" modifies "dollar" to indicate who the dollar belongs to.
