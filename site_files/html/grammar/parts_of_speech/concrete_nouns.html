Summary

Concrete nouns name things that have physical form or that would have physical form if they existed.

Explanation

Concrete nouns name things that have physical form or that would have physical form if they existed. Concrete nouns can be either common nouns or they can be proper nouns.

Examples

  1. A system of government that enables {money} to be accumulated by a few {people} in extreme disproportion to the distribution amongst all {people} will not survive.
  2. The seeds of revolution were planted in {France} long before the start of the Renaissance.
  3. {She} dreamed of {unicorns} that pondered the distinction between {up quarks} and {down quarks}.

Grammar Tips

Nouns that name fictional or theoretical objects, like unicorns or quarks are concrete rather than abstract nouns. However, "fantasy," "fiction," "myth," and "physics" are all abstract nouns that name ideas, concepts, or qualities associated with those things.
