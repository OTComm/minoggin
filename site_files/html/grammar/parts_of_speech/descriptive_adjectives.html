Summary

Descriptive adjectives modify nouns by naming an inherent or attributed characteristic that determines or identifies its kind or condition relative to others.

Explanation

Descriptive adjectives modify nouns by naming an inherent or attributed characteristic that determines or identifies its kind or condition relative to others. They answer the question “what kind?” That is, they clarify the noun’s qualities, distinguishing it from others by attributes such as size, color, condition, or other defining traits.

Examples

  1. The {rotten} apple spoils the bunch.
  2. The {old} woman laughed.
  3. They ran from the {deaf} dog.
