Summary

Transitive verbs represent action that is transmitted to a receiver. Trasitive active verbs are received by the direct object. Transitive passive verbs are recived by the subject.

Explanation

Transitive verbs have a sender and receiver. That is, the action indicated by the verb is carried from the the subject to the object.

  1. Transitive Active: Subject does action, and a direct object receives the action. Whenever there is a transitive active verb there is a direct object and vice-versa.
  2. Transitive Passive: Subject receives action of the verb. If the doer of the verb is shown, it is indicated with a preposition; for example, Bob was hit by a car.

Examples

  1. Helen {ate} the toast.
  2. The toast was {eatten} by Helen.
