Interjections

  • Parts of Speech
  • Interjections

Summary

Interjections are words or phrases used to express sudden emotions or reactions. They are often standalone and can convey feelings such as surprise, joy, pain, or excitement.

Explanation

Interjections are words or short phrases that convey strong emotions or sudden reactions and are typically used in spoken or informal written language. They are not grammatically related to other parts of the sentence, often standing alone or set off by punctuation like commas or exclamation marks. Interjections can express a range of emotions, such as surprise (“Wow!”), pain (“Ouch!”), happiness (“Yay!”), or frustration (“Ugh!”). They add emotional context and can influence the tone of a conversation or text.

Examples

  1. Wow! I didn’t expect that. (Surprise)
  2. Ouch! That hurt! (Pain)
  3. Yay! We won the game! (Happiness)
  4. Ugh, this traffic is terrible! (Frustration)
  5. Hooray! We’re going on vacation! (Excitement)
