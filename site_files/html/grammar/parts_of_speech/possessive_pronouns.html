Summary

Possessive pronouns indicate ownership or possession of nouns and are used to replace the nouns to which they refer.

Explanation

Possessive pronouns indicate ownership or possession of nouns and are used to replace the nouns to which they refer. Unlike Possessive Adjectives, possessive pronouns do not modify nouns directly. Rather, they stand in place of the nouns so that the noun itself does not need to be repeated.

Examples

  1. This car is {mine}, that car is {yours}, the car on the ramp is {his}, the blue car is {hers}, the car that has its wheels off is {ours} to share, and the car that was stolen was {theirs}.
  2. That black book is {mine}. (i.e., "The black book is my book." "My" is a possessive adjective directly modifying "book" to nindicate to whom the book belongs.
