Summary

Quantitative adjectives provide information about the quantity or ordinal position of a noun.

Explanation

Quantitative adjectives provide information about the quantity or ordinal position of a noun. Quantitative definite adjectives do this by using cardinal numbers to specify how many are being referred to (e.g., 1, 2, 3,...) or by using ordinal numbers to specify which ordered position a noun or set of nouns occupies (e.g., first, second, third,...). Quantitative indefinite adjectives do this by using words of indefinite approximation to indicate a relative sense of quantity (e.g., many, few, several, etc.).

Examples

  • Quantitative Definite
    1. I see {three} cookies.
    2. We got the {first} car off the assembly line.
  • Quantitative Indefinite
    1. There were {several} people who missed the show.
    2. {Many} dogs are great with children.

Grammar Tips

Some mistakenly refer to distributive adjectives as a type of quantitative adjective. However, significant distinctions exist: Quantitative adjectives quantify (count or estimate) nouns, answering "how many?" Distributive adjectives focus on distribution within a set, answering "which ones?"
