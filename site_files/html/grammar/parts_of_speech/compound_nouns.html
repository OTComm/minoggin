Summary

Compound nouns combine two or more words to create a new noun with a unique meaning.

Explanation

Compound nouns combine two or more words to create a new noun with a unique meaning. Some compound nouns are written as single word, some are written in hyphenated form, and some are written with the words remaining separate. They often represent objects or concepts that are best expressed through the combination of several words.

Examples

  1. Toothpaste: The two words with dramatically different meanings, "tooth" and "paste," are combined to create a new noun with its own unique meaning.
  2. Sister-in-law: The three words with dramatically different meanings, two nouns and one preposition, "sister," "in," and "law," are combined to create a new noun with its own unique meaning.
  3. Bus stop: The two words with dramatically different meanings, "bus" and "stop," remain separate but create a new noun with its own unique meaning.
