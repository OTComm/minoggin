Summary

Distributive adjectives specify individual members in a set or entire subsets within the larger set.

Explanation

Distributive adjectives specify individual members in a set or entire subsets within the larger set. They answer the question "which ones?" by indicating the distribution of nouns—whether people, places, things, or ideas. In other words, they refer to a precise portion of a set without generalizing (as indefinite quantitative adjectives do). Each distributive adjective isolates an element or elements for individual consideration, such as ‘each,’ ‘every,’ ‘either,’ and ‘neither.’”

Examples

  1. Of all those involved in the food fight, {each} boy was not allowed out for recess.
  2. {Neither} option sounds good.
  3. {Both} political parties answer to the same donors.
  4. You may {both} come along.

Grammar Tips

Some mistakenly refer to distributive adjectives as a type of quantitative adjective. However, significant distinctions exist: Quantitative adjectives quantify (count or estimate) nouns, answering "how many?" Distributive adjectives focus on distribution within a set, answering "which ones?"
