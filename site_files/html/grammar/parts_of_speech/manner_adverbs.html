Summary

Adverbs of manner explain "how" something is done, providing details about the behavior or method of an action.

Explanation

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs to answer the question when, where, why, how, how often, or how much. You might also think of them as telling you something about time, place, reason, method, frequency, and degree.

Examples

  1. She {already} changed the subject.
  2. He placed the locket {directly} above the picture.
  3. He {gently} placed the locket abover the picture.
  4. She {often} eats herring with lunch.
  5. Their reputation was {severely} diminished by agreeing to the terms.
