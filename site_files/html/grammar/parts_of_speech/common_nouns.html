Summary

Common nouns refer to generic categories of items or to generic items within a category.

Explanation

Common nouns refer to generic categories of items or to generic items within a category; they do not refer to specific names and titles. Common nouns are distinct from Proper Nouns, but they are not distinct from Concrete Nouns and Abstract Nouns; rather, Common Nouns and Abstract Nouns are the two top-level sets of noun types. In other words, all common nouns are either concrete or abstract.

Examples

  1. Henry Wilson is the {doctor} who delivered her {baby}.
  2. The {man} who delivered her {baby} was Doctor Henry Wilson.
  3. They have escaped with the {president} and Vice President Johnson.
  4. They have escaped with President Wilson and the {vice president}.
  5. The {theory} he discussed is not at all like the {theories} we're used to.
