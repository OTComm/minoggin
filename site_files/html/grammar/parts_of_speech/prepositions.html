Prepostions

  • Parts of Speech
  • Prepositions

Summary

Prepositions are words that show the relationship between nouns (or pronouns) and other words in a sentence.

Explanation

Prepositions can indicate time, location, direction, or other abstract relationships. Prepositions are typically short words and are used to express the connection between different elements within the sentences.

Examples

  1. {On} Monday, we'll see the results. (Time)
  2. She placed the book {on} the table. (Location)
  3. He walked {toward} the park. (Direction)
  4. They were talking {about} the movie. (Topic)
  5. He did it {for} her. (Purpose)
  6. The painting {by} Monet is exquisite. (Agent)
  7. The cup is {on} the table. (Position)
  8. She arrived {after} the meeting started. (Time relation)
