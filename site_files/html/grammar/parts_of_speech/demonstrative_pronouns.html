Summary

Demonstrative pronouns point to and identify a noun or a pronoun.

Explanation

Demonstrative pronouns point to and identify a noun or a pronoun. As the name suggests, they demonstrate the noun they refer to. Demonstrative pronouns include, "this," "that," "these," and "those." Sometimes "neither," "none," and "such" are also used as demonstrative pronouns. Unlike [[Demonstrative Adjectives]], they do not modify the noun; rather, they stand in place of the noun to avoid repetition.

Examples

  1. {This} doesn't look quite as good as {that}, but if {these} were expensive, imagine how much {those} cost. (Here each demonstrative pronoun stands in place of the noun to avoid repetition.)
  2. {This} pizza doesn't look quite as good as {that} pizza, but if {these} seats were expensive, imagine how much {those} seats cost. (Here the same words function as demonstrative adjectives, modifying the nouns that follow them.)
  3. I liked the way they looked, but {those} were not the ones I wanted.
