Summary

Compound adjectives are composed of two or more words that work together to modify a noun.

Explanation

Compound adjectives are formed by combining two or more words in a hyphenated string where the words in the string sequentially modify eachother to form a single modifier of a noun. This is distinct from cumulative adjectives, which are not hyphenated and no comma is used between then (whereas coordinate adjectives do require a comma).

Examples

  1. {Middle-aged} man
  2. {too-busy-wathcing-football-to-have-any-idea-about-what-is-actually-happening-in-the-world} voters.
  3. {Blue-eyed} girl
  4. It was a {bitter-sweet} moment.
  5. They entered a {sparsely-populated} room.
