Summary

Gerunds are nouns that are formed by adding "ing" to the end of a verb *and* using it in the position of a noun in a sentence.

Explanation

Gerunds are nouns that are formed by adding "ing" to the end of a verb *and* using it in the position of a noun in a sentence. In other words, adding "ing" to a verb does not automatically make it a gerund; it must also be used as a noun.

Examples

  1. {Fishing} is relaxing.
  2. He enjoys {fishing} more than he enjoys himself.
  3. Do you mind my {asking} you?
